PART 1 — 單字 Vocabulary
1The word "surpass" means ___.字義
"Messi netted his 17th World Cup goal, surpassing the long-standing record of 16 goals previously shared with Germany's Miroslav Klose."
✅ Correct: B — surpass = exceed, go beyond. Root: sur-(over) + pass(go through). Synonyms: exceed, outdo, top.
2In the sentence, "cement" is used figuratively. What does it mean?字義(比喻)
"Messi cemented his legacy as the greatest goalscorer in World Cup history."
✅ Correct: B — cement (figurative) = make solid/permanent. Like concrete binding bricks, it means to establish firmly.
3The word "legacy" in sports context refers to ___.字義
"At 38, Messi continues to defy expectations, defining his legacy in the sport."
✅ Correct: C — legacy = lasting achievement or influence (not necessarily money). Common collocations: leave a legacy, lasting legacy.
4What does "resignation" mean politically?字義
"British PM Keir Starmer announced his resignation, making him the country's seventh leader in a decade."
✅ Correct: A — resignation = voluntarily giving up a position (vs removal = forced out). Collocations: hand in one's resignation.
5"caretaker" describes someone who ___.字義
"Starmer will remain as caretaker prime minister until a new Labour leader is chosen."
✅ Correct: C — caretaker = temporary holder of a role. Collocations: caretaker government, caretaker manager.
6Which best explains "frontrunner"?同義詞
"Andy Burnham is considered the strong frontrunner to replace Starmer."
✅ Correct: B — frontrunner = leading candidate, most likely to win. Root: front + runner = runs ahead of all others.
7In international relations, "sanctions" are ___.字義
"The framework includes discussions on sanctions relief and the release of frozen Iranian assets."
✅ Correct: C — sanctions = punitive measures like trade restrictions to pressure a country. Collocations: impose sanctions, lift sanctions, sanctions relief.
8The verb "broker" is closest in meaning to ___.同義詞
"The framework was brokered with the assistance of Qatar and Pakistan."
✅ Correct: D — broker (verb) = negotiate/arrange. Think stock broker (middleman) — broker a deal, broker a peace agreement.
9"incumbent" before a political title means the person ___.字義
"The result marks a sharp turn away from the policies of incumbent President Gustavo Petro."
✅ Correct: A — incumbent = currently holding the position. Note: the incumbent often has an advantage in elections.
10A candidate's "platform" in politics refers to ___.字義(政治)
"De la Espriella campaigned on a platform prioritizing economic stability and law enforcement."
✅ Correct: B — platform = a politician's set of policies and goals. Collocations: campaign on a platform of..., policy platform.
PART 2 — 文法 Grammar
11Choose the sentence with correct present perfect tense.現在完成式
"Messi netted his 17th World Cup goal, becoming the all-time leading goalscorer."
✅ Correct: B — Present perfect = has/have + Vpp. Used for past events with current relevance. A/C/D have tense conflicts.
12Which correctly transforms the sentence into passive voice?被動語態
Active: "Qatar and Pakistan brokered the deal in Switzerland."
✅ Correct: B — Passive = Object + was/were + Vpp + by + Subject. A: missing by. C: broker should be brokered. D: active subject can't be passivized this way.
13Choose the correct relative pronoun.關係代名詞
"Andy Burnham, ___ recently returned to Parliament, is the frontrunner to replace Starmer."
✅ Correct: C — who = refers to a person, used as subject of the relative clause (___ returned). Rule: who=subject, whom=object, which=things, whose=possessive.
14Which correctly uses the first conditional?條件句
"Iran may suspend uranium enrichment. In return, the U.S. may lift some sanctions."
✅ Correct: C — First conditional = If + present simple, will/may + base verb. B is wrong: never use will in the if-clause! A=2nd conditional, D=3rd conditional.
15Convert to indirect speech (reported speech).間接引語
Direct: Starmer said, "I will resign and step aside for the good of the party."
✅ Correct: B — Backshift rule: when reporting verb is past (said), shift tenses back: will→would, can→could, is→was. A: will must become would. C: tell needs an object (tell sb that). D: resigned changes meaning.
16Which uses a participial phrase correctly?分詞構句
Original: "Because Messi scored twice, he broke the all-time World Cup scoring record."
✅ Correct: C — When the action happens BEFORE the main action, use Having + Vpp. Rules: simultaneous=V-ing, prior=Having+Vpp, passive=Being/Having been+Vpp.
17Choose the correct concessive expression.讓步連接詞
Idea 1: Messi is in his late 30s. Idea 2: He continues to perform at the highest level.
✅ Correct: C — Although+clause (no but!). Despite+noun/gerund. However is an adverb, not a conjunction. Key rule: Although never pairs with but!
18Identify the appositive phrase and what it refers to.同位語
"Andy Burnham, the popular former Mayor of Greater Manchester, is the frontrunner to replace Starmer."
✅ Correct: B — Appositive = noun phrase after a noun that renames/explains it, set off by commas. Test: remove it — sentence still works. Andy Burnham is the frontrunner — still complete!
19Choose the best transition expression for the Iran deal exchange.轉折副詞
"Iran agreed to invite IAEA inspectors back. ___, the U.S. is expected to lift some sanctions."
✅ Correct: B — In return = showing mutual exchange/reciprocity. Nevertheless=concession, As a result=cause-effect, On the contrary=opposition.
20Choose the sentence with correct past perfect tense.過去完成式
"Many Labour MPs had grown deeply concerned before Starmer announced his resignation."
✅ Correct: B — Past perfect (had+Vpp) = event that happened before another past event. By the time + past simple, S + had already + Vpp. A: missing had. C: have grown is present perfect. D: will have grown is future perfect.
